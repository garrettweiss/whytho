# WhyTho — Key Talking Points

## The One-Sentence Pitch
"WhyTho is the permanent public record of whether your representative actually answers your questions — whether or not they show up."

## The Core Insight
Non-response is a choice. Politicians have always been able to stay silent and have that silence disappear into the noise. WhyTho makes silence permanent and public, the same way a voting record is permanent and public.

## Three Things That Make It Different

**1. It works without the politician**
Most civic tech platforms require elected officials to opt in. WhyTho creates a permanent accountability record regardless. A 0% response rate is just as informative as 100%.

**2. Community surfaces what matters**
Individual questions get lost. The upvote system means constituents collectively decide which questions actually represent community priorities — not one loud voice.

**3. The score follows them forever**
This isn't a one-time campaign. Response rates accumulate week over week, term over term. A politician can't have a bad year and then reset. The record is permanent.

---

## Why This Moment

- Most Republican members of Congress refused in-person town halls in 2025
- Constituents organizing outside empty offices — the frustration is real and documented
- No existing tool creates a permanent, public, standardized accountability record
- The gap between constituent expectations and representative engagement is at a decade high

---

## What We're Not

- **Not a petition platform** — petitions disappear. Response rates are permanent.
- **Not partisan** — identical standards for all 537 federal officials
- **Not an AI impersonation tool** — AI-generated content is always clearly labeled; never presented as coming from the politician
- **Not a gotcha machine** — politicians who answer well get high response rates. Participation is rewarded.

---

## The Business Model (if asked)
WhyTho is pre-revenue. We're focused on building the public record infrastructure first. Future revenue paths include: verification fees for politicians, civic data licensing, and premium features for journalists and researchers.
