# WhyTho — Press One-Pager

**The platform where political silence is on the record.**

---

## What It Is

WhyTho is a free, nonpartisan civic accountability platform. Constituents submit questions for their elected officials. The community upvotes the ones that matter most. Questions that reach 10+ votes become "qualifying" — ones the politician is publicly expected to answer before the week ends.

Every Monday, the week resets. Each politician's response rate is locked in permanently. Whether they answered or stayed silent, it's on their public record forever.

**Silence is its own answer.**

---

## The Problem It Solves

Town halls are disappearing. Congressional offices screen calls. Social media is a broadcast channel, not a dialogue. Constituents have no reliable, public, permanent record of whether their representative actually engages with them.

WhyTho creates that record — automatically, every week, whether or not the politician participates.

---

## How It Works

1. **Anyone can ask** — free account, no verification required for constituents
2. **Community decides what matters** — upvotes surface the most important questions
3. **Qualifying threshold is 10+ net votes** — filters noise, focuses pressure
4. **Politicians can respond directly** — verified officials answer in their own voice
5. **Weekly reset every Monday** — response rate is recalculated and permanently recorded
6. **Non-response is never hidden** — a 0% response rate is just as visible as 100%

---

## Key Facts

- **Scope:** 537 federal officials + all governors + state legislators in 10 states
- **Built by:** Quinnivations LLC (independent, nonpartisan)
- **Status:** Pre-launch, pilot in Colorado's 8th Congressional District (March 2026)
- **URL:** whytho-alpha.vercel.app
- **Verification:** Politicians can claim and verify profiles (government email, FEC ID, or other methods)
- **AI use:** AI-generated suggested questions are always clearly labeled as such. AI analysis of public record is never presented as a statement from the politician.
- **Data:** All response rates are public and permanent — no takebacks

---

## Why Now

Constituent engagement has collapsed. Most Republican members of Congress have refused to hold in-person town halls in 2025. Constituents are organizing outside empty offices. The accountability gap is at a decade high — and WhyTho builds the permanent public record that fills it.
